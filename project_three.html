ARR CLIENT BRIDGE

DYNAMIC ANALYSIS OUT OF A CSV FILE

Use-case

For this project, I’ve assisted my client (the management team of the Target business) in preparing their business for sale (Vendor Due Diligence). The Target business is a global provider or technology and talent solutions which had operations in the UK, US, India, and Dubai.

On this assignement, I've been given direct access to my client's accounting system to extract and transform data that were not analysed historically by the business nor readily available from their outsourced accountants. This enabled us to create all the key analysis required in this particular sector, ie 1) revenue bridge by client, 2) client concentration, 3) top customer analysis, and 4) cohort analysis.

After reviewing the existing data in the business and the systems in place, I figured out that the best file to use for our analysis was a CSV extract which contained all sort of information on billings which were recorded over the Historical Period.

Once I’ve established the data requirements, I have performed the necessary cleansing in an Alteryx workflow to prepare the historical data for a consistent and accurate reporting. I have assessed suitable data segmentation (e.g. by billing entity, revenue stream, and product type) and any potential for data enrichment (e.g., sector or region). Using my financial due diligence expertise, I have then reviewed the revenue streams to accurately determine a view of recurring vs repeating vs one off revenue and apply those judgements to our Alteryx workflow.

Solution

I've created a flat file out of the CSV extract, so that the data could then be stored and modelled in Power Pivot.

Storing data in Power Pivot allows us to create tables and charts by directly linking them to Pivot tables (using the GETPIVOTDATA formula). This is the key to create any dynamic data-book in Excel, as this will allow you to bring up slicers or timeline that will update the output (table/visuals) based on the chosen data categorisation.

The final output was a dynamic data-book, built in Excel, which included the following analysis:

  • Historical monthly revenue, cut by both ARR value and customer numbers, along with associated KPIs. Movements have been bucketed appropriately, including customer churn, downsell, new customer, and updsell. Each revenue movement bucket has been presented dynamically so the customer make up can be analysed and a matrix shown to split each movement bucket by size.
  • Top customer and customer cohort analysis along with the monthly evolution of that cohort both on a logo and revenue value basis.
  • Product stratification analysis showing the number of customers taking each product category and the evolution over time.

Key takeaways

⚡ Efficiency gains

The real power of using Power Pivot instead of scratch pads lies in the efficiency gains achieved from repeatability. If your data changes, you can just re-run the Alteryx workflow and refresh the Power Pivot and all of your analysis will update automatically.

⚡ File size

Storing data in Power Pivot rather than scratch pads drastically reduces the file size.
